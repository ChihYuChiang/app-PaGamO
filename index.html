3 things to make PaGamO even better

  1. More meaningful rewards
  2. Smoother early-stage experience
  3. Easily swappable visual style and elements

played and tested in mid-August

1. More meaningful rewards

  •   better single-player experience
  •   stickier experience, with more applicable contexts

1. More meaningful rewards

Meaningful rewards

  • Rewards that provide value to the players. Rewards that the players care about and are willing to pursue.
  • Competition (and winning the competition) between the players, especially between acquaintances in the real world, is meaningful.
  • An ATK attribute of an in-game character itself is not meaningful.
  • An ATK attribute of an in-game character which helps the players winning the game is not meaningful.
  • It is meaningful only when the players care about winning the game and are aware of the connection between winning the game and the ATK attribute.

Many in-game rewards (resources) in PaGamO, such as the character’s ATK attribute, are not obviously meaningful to the players and can be improved.

Create meaningful rewards

  • Set up clear goals (winning conditions).
    EG A prosperous land/kingdom, a better ranking.
  • Design rewards, i.e. money, energy, character attributes, connected to the goals.
  • Make sure the players know the goals and care about the goals.
  • Make sure the players know the connections between the goals and the rewards.

1. More meaningful rewards

Implementation examples

  • Implement explanations (connections) as hover tips for each attribute/item.
    EG How does it influence the battles/question answering?
  • For important resources, such as Money, Energy, and Land, provide dedicated tutorials.
    EG What can I do with a land? The purpose of acquiring lands?
  • Provide explicit linkages to the goals when the resource is making its effect.
    EG Show computation of the damage to an opponent based on the ATK value of a character.

Gain from meaningful rewards

  • With the already strong multi-player experience, more meaningful rewards reinforce PaGamO’s single-player experience.
  • With better single-player experience, PaGamO is easier to be applied in contexts outside organizations where interpersonal competitions are weaker.
    EG English learning of an entire country.
  • Better single-player experience creates additional fun and value to a player, and PaGamO becomes stickier. This should especially improve the platform’s short-term attrition rates. EG 30-min, 1-day.

2. Smoother early-stage experience

  •   demonstrate value and fun
  •   easier to get on board, facilitating business development

2. Smoother early-stage experience

Early-stage experience

  • The first ~10 minutes in the game. Often featured with the highest attrition rate.
  • When the players are unfamiliar with the game.
  • When the unfamiliarity creates a high hurdle for players really enjoying and involving in the activity (gameplay).
  • Two main focuses of the early-stage experience:
    • Educate the players the game’s rules (how to play) and goals (what to achieve).
    • Demonstrate to the players the value and fun behind the rules and goals.

It requires a longer time to learn PaGamO by playing around by the players themselves, especially without a facilitator beside the play sessions. PaGamO’s rules, goals, and value can be conveyed more clearly.

Create smoother early-stage experience

  • Design better tutorials—tutorials have to make clear points about how to play and about the goals in the game.
  • During the early-stage, limit the information shown to the players, making the learning more focused.
  • Make the introduction process itself a meaningful reward to the players. EG A new way to expand my land!

2. Smoother early-stage experience

Implementation examples

  • Mask not-current-focus elements and introduce with tutorial step by step.
    EG Teach land expansion mechanisms (attack the bordered lands) without other players in sight.
  • Provide a smaller area (sea) to explore when start and increase as progresses.
    EG Start with 5*5 cells.
  • Demonstrate later-stage experience, to present what the players could achieve and the fun the players could acquire.
    EG Several beautiful and highly-developed kingdoms prosperous and colorful.

Gain from smoother early-stage experience

  • A smoother early-stage experience reduces PaGamO players’ learning cost, shortening the period of unfamiliarity.
  • Players are easier to get on board, enjoy the activity, and really acquire value from PaGamO.
  • The value and fun of PaGamO are better demonstrated; this lowers the entry barrier and facilitates the communication in business development.
  • The improvements should especially benefit the platform’s instant attrition rates. EG 5-min, 15-min.

3. Easily swappable visual style and elements

  •   cater to distinct tastes of variant audiences
  •   more marketable to different organizations

3. Easily swappable visual style and elements

Swappable visual style and elements

  • Obviously, different people prefer different visual styles.
    EG Cute Japanese style, futuristic style.
  • Visual attraction is one of the top reasons of forming product preferences (I like this game, but not the other one).
  • Product visual style is connected to an user’s/player’s personal style and self-conscientiousness. When the two styles are aligned, the users/players gain positive perception toward that product.
  • In addition, pleasurable visual style itself can be a meaningful reward to the players.
  • If a product’s visual style is swappable, the product gains an useful feature to appeal to the users/players; otherwise, the product could be limited.

While PaGamO set to be a general platform for learning, a fixed visual style could hamper its opportunity with different types of players (learners).

Approaches

  • Start from the core items:
    • Avatar, land, ocean    UI    Items and equipment.
  • Create several in-house visual sets (themes) to be sold with the platform.
  • Reserve the flexibility for the client institution (develop own visual elements according to the client’s needs).

3. Easily swappable visual style and elements

Implementation examples

  • Prepare multiple default themes can be applied in the admin console of the institutional clients.
  • In addition, prepare a folder, with templates (prefabs) of all swappable elements.
  • Client institutions are allowed to swap the elements for what they believe their customers would like, only with certain object specifications. EG Dimension, file size.
  • The visual elements can be created by individual players.
  • This element-bounty events can serve as a form of marketing campaign of the PaGamO platform.

Gain from the swappable visual style and elements

  • PaGamO caters to distinct tastes of variant audiences.
    EG Elementary school students and adults.
  • Because the different tastes are catered, PaGamO acquires a generally better customer perception.
  • PaGamO is more marketable to different organizations.
    EG Elementary schools and banks.
